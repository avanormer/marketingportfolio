AI Content Experiments

A role-aligned project exploring how AI image generation and emerging marketing tools can support sharper B2B content, stronger visual storytelling, and more efficient creative workflows.

Core Experiment Insight

AI-generated visuals are most useful when they are treated as strategic creative tools — not decoration. For B2B companies like Sparq, the strongest AI visuals should clarify complex ideas, support thought leadership, and make technical topics feel more accessible without becoming generic.

Experiment Objective

Sparq’s role specifically values curiosity around AI tools and emerging marketing technologies. This project is designed to show how I would test AI-generated visuals for LinkedIn posts, blog headers, campaign concepts, and thought leadership content.

01

Prompt Strategy

Test how different prompt styles affect the quality, tone, and usefulness of AI-generated visuals.

02

Brand Fit

Evaluate whether visuals feel credible for an enterprise AI and digital engineering audience.

03

Content Use Cases

Explore how AI images could support LinkedIn posts, blog thumbnails, carousel concepts, and campaign graphics.

04

Quality Control

Identify what should be refined, rejected, or edited before AI visuals are used in public marketing content.

How I Would Run the Experiment

Step 1

Choose one B2B concept, such as production-ready AI, operational efficiency, workflow modernization, or connected data systems.

Step 2

Generate multiple visual directions using different prompt styles: abstract, editorial, executive, technical, and human-centered.

Step 3

Evaluate each output based on clarity, professionalism, brand fit, visual originality, and usefulness for LinkedIn or blog content.

Step 4

Document recommendations on which visuals should be used, revised, avoided, or turned into future campaign concepts.

Evaluation Framework

Criteria

What I’d Look For

Why It Matters

Clarity

Does the visual help explain the idea, or does it make the concept feel more vague?

B2B visuals should support understanding, not distract from the message.

Credibility

Does the image feel polished, professional, and appropriate for an enterprise audience?

AI content can quickly feel cheap if the visual style does not match the brand’s authority.

Specificity

Does the output feel connected to Sparq’s world of AI, data, systems, and operational performance?

Generic AI imagery weakens differentiation and makes the brand blend in.

Usability

Could this visual realistically support a LinkedIn post, blog header, or campaign asset?

The best AI experiments should lead to practical marketing recommendations.
